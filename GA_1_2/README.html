A Quick Recap

Last week we focused on investigating some of the available data and identifying possible models for predicting the break-up date in the Nenana Ice Classic. We looked at the results of a few models and realized that a large part of the model error was due to variability in the data. Most of this is associated with aleatoric uncertainty due to the somewhat-random behavior of nature. This week we will focus in particular on the uncertainty in ice growth, since that has a direct effect on when and how the tripod falls over and moves downstream.

As a reminder:

  • Each year you can bet on the day and time the river ice in the Tanana River, Alaska, will break apart along the waterfront of the town Nenana.
  • A tripod is constructed on the ice during the first weekend in March.
  • Break-up time is determined as follows: "The Tripod is setup with a unique pulley and clock system that stops the clock once the tripod has moved downstream by 100 ft."
  • You can buy a ticket for $3 and place a bet between Feb 1 and April 5.
  • Visit the website for more information, or to view a live webcam of the river nenanaakiceclassic.com.
